- [3/9/26] I spent a lot of time the past few days in class and at home figuring out what I want to do. I found a RepRap project called FoldaRap that I will be taking inspiration from. I have found sources for most of my parts and have a design in mind for the heated bed. It will be 220x220 mm and will be connected as such: MGN12 linear rail -> MGN12C carriage -> y-axis carriage plate (either CNC'd or sourced from an existing printer) -> insulation stuck to heater stuck to aluminum plate with magnetic sheet adhered on top to hold the PEI build plate. I will be using linear rails for all of the axes and will have the x-axis fold up instead of the complicated y-axis folding mechanism I was previously envisioning. As I type this, I am designing the heated bed in CAD, as I am creating the individual components and then constraining them into an assembly in Fusion. (7 hours).

- [3/13/26] I have finished the Y-axis assembly. It will slide back and forth onto the briefcase wall for transportation. I have finalized my BOM and purchased the AliExpress components so that they get here on time. I still have not secured the salvage 3D printers, but I am hoping to get them this weekend. It took a while to make the y-axis assembly reasonably compact, but I think I did it. The main troubles were getting the stepper to go under the bed as it moved. This meant making the y axis carriage plate with enough space for the stepper while still letting the bed go on top of it. I also had to get the pulleys aligned with the belt tensioner on the 3D printed y axis carriage. This took a few hours of back and forth between different fusion workspaces and waiting for it to update so I could see if it worked. Speaking of the belt tensioner, I found a STEP model online someone developed, but I completely overhauled it, changing the side widths and attaching it to the y-axis carriage plate. I want to make this y-axis as compact as possible, so I want the carriage plate nearly touching the stepper. I was able to get the length down to 375mm, which is in my opinion still too long, as this length determines the height of the final briefcase. (6.5 hours)

  I started by changing my fan. Anicetus led me to the realization that a 4020 is too big for this toolhead, so I switched the design to use a 3010. I then discussed with Anicetus and Allen on the infill slack for ideas. I ended up deciding to angle the fans at 20 degrees and add a cooling duct for the part cooling fans. I ended up deciding on a duct output size of 5.5x10ishmm, since 4010s have decent static pressure. I spent a lot of time angling it correctly (following this video https://youtu.be/YijwkQCOBEA?si=ugOIRSkkFT1ZPhZg) and also added the mounting spots for the fans. They will slot into place and then will screw in at the top with a m3 screw into some heated inserts. I also extruded the bottom of the duct flat so that it will not run into the bed. I also discussed the use of a klicky probe, and looked at this video https://www.youtube.com/shorts/k0mwwjaCt78 to get an idea of how I am going to mount it. I didn't get to mounting it today, but I am thinking about potentially using a servo to take it on and off. (6 hours)  

- [3/21/26] For today's work, I did a lot of work on the final assembly. It is a weekend, so I was able to sit down and work for eight hours straight. I was able to nearly finalize the z axis stepper mounts, and struggled a lot with belt routing. I started by designing the spots for the linear bearings and the lead screw nut. I took a close look at the Prusa MK3 which I imported into Fusion (https://www.printables.com/model/179638-original-prusa-i3-mk3-for-fusion-360and-stl) and figured that I would munt the rails and rods a little differently. I was initally going to have them in line with the x axis extrsion, but I could not get this to work for my design, as it was not compact enough. So, I found a picture of another design which had the linear rod behind the lead screw. I modified the stepper mount to have the lead screw nut and the linear rod mount in a different position closer to each other, similar to the Prusa but with the rod behind the lead screw instead of next to it. I also added to this a mount for the x axis extrusion which was difficult to get right because I needed it precicely aligned. Once I got that designed, I had to make the stepper motor mount. I followed the design philosophy of the LH Stinger 3d printer (https://cad.onshape.com/documents/298df90608f84bebaadf0a1d/w/e2b1d7481338cfb2dcad3d5f/e/bf98cd8552c9573200b042ea) and made everything printable without supports. However, this was only after a few hour struggle where I couldn't figure out how to attach the x axis motor to the linear rod and rail mount. I just could not get it to work, and eventually started from the first extrsuion and worked my way from there. I had to move a lot of stuff around, but eventually got it to work. (8 hours)

- [4/7/26-4/14/26] Building! This took a lot of time. I started by 3D printing out some of the parts, then realized that I forgot to add tolerances on several of them. It took around 4 revisions before I found tolerances that worked for the stepper mount and the m5 bolts. I also realized that I had forgotten to modify the y axis motion system to use 9mm belt rather than 6mm belt. So, I redesigned the tensioning system from scratch to make it work for 10mm belt. After that was sorted, I tried to print out the y axis carriage plate out of PAHT-CF. This did not go well, as I had problems with adhesion and warping, so I switched to ABS. I also enlisted the help of my dad when cutting and drilling the metal. The main bottom plate took two attempts, and we cut and drilled based on a fusion file I had exported and printed in four sheets to create a drilling template. We did this for all 3 of the plates. I then screwed everything in and realized I forgot to design for the hinges. So, we painstakingly drilled out all of the holes for the miniscule hinges until we got it to work. This is around the time I designed the servo mount for the klicky probe integreation and printed up the rest of the pieces. I learned how to use heated inserts, but I do not have the correct soldering iron tip, so a lot of the time the insert got clogged with plastic and I had to redo it. I also had to fix the design for the z axis rod mount, as I had no way of clamping it in place. I ended up using heated inserts to hold a part which clamped the rod to the mount. I also had to design a part to hold the walls of the briefcase up when folding, as I ran out of time to drill the latches into place. This is also when I attached the handle to the top. All together, this stage of building took around (20 hours).
